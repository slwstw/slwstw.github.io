SLWS Field Survey in Mongolia Supports Long-term Forest Restoration

    SLWS recently participated in a field survey in northern Mongolia. The fieldwork was conducted in a forest-steppe landscape to support a long-term forest restoration project. The team collected data on soil properties, biodiversity, biomass, water quality, and habitat conditions. The field experience provided valuable opportunities to connect GIS, ecology, and environmental research with real-world observations while gaining a deeper understanding of Mongolia’s unique landscapes and ecosystems.

    研究室的成員們前往蒙古北部進行野外調查，調查內容涵蓋土壤、生物多樣性、生物量、水質與棲地條件等面向，評估不同條件下的森林種植潛力，為長期復森林計畫的建立基礎。

SLWS Research on Afforestation Climate Impact and Heat Mortality Featured at EGU

    Research Assistant Yu-Hsiang Chang and Master's student Shao-Fang Li traveled to Vienna, Austria, for the European Geosciences Union General Assembly (EGU 2026). Yu-Hsiang presented on afforestation's climate impacts in Mongolia, and Shao-Fang discussed a quantitative model analyzing mortality risk from extreme temperatures in Taiwan.

    本研究室助理張淯翔與碩士生李劭方於 2026 年 5 月前往奧地利維也納，參加 2026 歐洲地球科學聯盟年會（European Geosciences Union General Assembly）。EGU 年會是地球科學領域最具指標性與規模最大的國際會議之一，在為期 5 天的會議期間，匯聚了超過 2 萬名來自世界各地的專家學者，共同展示與交流最新的研究成果與技術發展。

    本次會議中，兩人發表之研究皆由翁葳老師指導。張淯翔以 "Climate impacts from afforestation in temperate regions: a regional assessment in Mongolia" 為題進行口頭發表，探討溫帶地區造林於減緩全球暖化之氣候效應評估；李劭方則進行海報發表，主題為 "Adaptation to warming climate: analyzing minimum mortality temperature in Taiwan"，建立量化模型以探討臺灣極端氣溫事件與致死風險的關係。此次遠赴 EGU 發表的經驗，不僅提供了與國際研究者交流的寶貴機會，亦大幅拓展了學術視野。

    Reference:
    Chang, Y.-H. and Weng, W.: Climate impacts of afforestation in temperate regions: a regional assessment in Mongolia, EGU General Assembly 2026, Vienna, Austria, 3-8 May 2026, EGU26-17876, https://doi.org/10.5194/egusphere-egu26-17876, 2026.
    Li, S.-F., Chen, Z.-F., and Weng, W.: Adaptation to warming climate: analyzing minimum mortality temperature in Taiwan, EGU General Assembly 2026, Vienna, Austria, 3-8 May 2026, EGU26-19441, https://doi.org/10.5194/egusphere-egu26-19441, 2026.

Hao-Ren Shiau Delivers Research on Amazon Water Livelihoods in Birmingham

    In August 2025, our research assistant Hao-Ren Shiau delivered an oral presentation at the RGS-IBG Annual International Conference. His research, supervised by Prof. Wei Weng and titled "Not Just Numbers: Water-Based Livelihood in a Deforested Amazonian Landscape," analyzed household survey data and discussed how local Amazonian communities manage essential water-based livelihoods while confronting severe hydroclimate events.

    本研究室助理蕭浩任於 2025 年 8 月前往英國伯明罕，於皇家地理學會年度國際會議（RGS-IBG Annual International Conference 2025）進行口頭發表。該研究由翁葳老師指導，題目為 Not Just Numbers: Water-Based Livelihood in a Deforested Amazonian Landscape，研究透過分析家戶調查資料，探討在地以水為基礎的生活，其挑戰與永續解方。
